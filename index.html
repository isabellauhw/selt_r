Imagine you are starting a new academic year in a new class. As you settle into your new classroom, you notice the diverse group of students around you.

As you start to interact with your peers, you form impressions of them, whether you might get on with them, and what they will be like as classmates. Other people in the class will also be making impressions of you. Since it is a new class, you haven't met your classmates before.

Your task is to figure out what your classmates think of you and each other. By the end you will need to decide who you would most like to sit next to in class for the rest of the year.

For some of your classmates, you might get a second chance to figure out what they think of you. We will give you one practice trial to show you how you will figure out what they think.

You'll be given a series of word pairs. In each pair, you must decide how likely it is that would use one or the other word to describe . Please indicate how likely you think this is on the scale between the two words.
